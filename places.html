Tourist Places of Hassan District

Browse key attractions across Hassan—heritage temples, spiritual hills, scenic ruins, and reservoirs.

Belur – Chennakesava Temple

A masterpiece of Hoysala architecture (12th century), famed for its star-shaped plan, soapstone sculptures, and ornate friezes. The temple complex celebrates fine iconography and living heritage.

Location: Belur town Timing: 6:30 AM – 8:00 PM

Tip: Hire a certified guide to decode the carvings; photography etiquette in sanctum.

View images View location

Halebidu – Hoysaleswara Temple

Twin shrines dedicated to Shiva, showcasing elaborate panels and figurines that narrate epics and local lore. A UNESCO tentative list site reflecting the zenith of Hoysala stone craft.

Location: Halebidu Timing: 6:30 AM – 8:00 PM

Tip: Visit Belur and Halebidu together; early mornings offer soft light for photos.

View images View location

Shravanabelagola – Bahubali Statue

A revered Jain pilgrimage with the 17-metre monolithic statue of Gomateshwara atop Vindhyagiri. The site hosts the Mahamastakabhisheka festival every 12 years.

Location: Channarayapatna taluk Steps: ~600 to summit

Tip: Carry water and start early; footwear not allowed on the climb.

View images View location

Shettihalli Rosary Church

Gothic-style church ruins that submerge seasonally due to the Hemavathi Reservoir. A photographer’s delight with dramatic waterscapes across monsoon and winter.

Location: Near Shettihalli village Best: Monsoon & winter

Tip: Check water levels before travel; evenings are great for silhouettes.

View images View location

Manjarabad Fort

A unique star-shaped hill fort built by Tipu Sultan, offering sweeping views of the Western Ghats. Known for its strategic geometry and breezy vantage points.

Location: Near Sakleshpur Timing: 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Tip: Avoid mid-day heat; roads can be misty during monsoon—drive carefully.

View images View location

Gorur Dam

Built across the Hemavathi River, the dam creates serene backwaters and picnic spots. Seasonal water release offers a dramatic sight.

Location: Hassan–Arkalgud road Season: Post-monsoon vistas

Tip: Follow safety barriers; check local timings for viewpoint access.

Seege Gudda

A tranquil hill viewpoint popular for short hikes and sunset panoramas near Hassan. Rolling greenery and gentle breezes make it a relaxing nature stop.

Location: Near Hassan Best: Sunrise/Sunset

Tip: Wear comfortable shoes; carry water and keep the hillside litter-free.

Parvathamma Betta

A serene hillock with wide-open views and calm surroundings. Ideal for quick getaways, light treks, and quiet photography sessions.

Location: Hassan region Terrain: Gentle climb

Tip: Early mornings are cooler; avoid disturbing local flora and fauna.

Haluvagilu

A quiet village ambience with glimpses of traditional architecture and slow countryside life in Hassan district. Good for mindful, responsible rural tourism.

Location: Hassan district Vibe: Rural & cultural

Tip: Be respectful of residents and ask permission before photographing people or homes.

Hasanamba Temple

A revered temple dedicated to Goddess Hasanamba, uniquely opened to devotees for a brief period annually. The festival period draws large pilgrim gatherings.

Location: Hassan city Open: During annual festival

Tip: Check dates well in advance; expect queues during the festival window.

Lakshmi Narasimha Temple, Nuggehalli

A 13th-century Hoysala temple with exquisite stone detailing and balanced proportions. A must-visit for admirers of Hoysala craftsmanship.

Location: Nuggehalli Era: 13th century

Tip: Combine with nearby Hoysala sites for a rich architectural circuit.

Ishvara Temple, Arasikere

Noted Hoysala-era shrine dedicated to Shiva, featuring a distinctive stellate plan and detailed iconography across friezes and pillars.

Location: Arasikere Style: Hoysala

Tip: Mid-morning light helps reveal fine surface carvings for photos.

Bucesvara Temple, Koravangala

A 12th-century Shiva temple renowned for its ornate doorways, finely carved ceilings, and historically significant inscriptions.

Location: Koravangala Era: 12th century

Tip: Respect temple decorum; avoid touching sculptures.

Ramanathapura Temple
