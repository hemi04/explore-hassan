Travel Guide

All you need to plan a comfortable and memorable visit within Hassan District.

How to Reach Hassan

By Road

Hassan is well-connected via NH-75 from Bengaluru (~185 km) and Mangaluru (~170 km). Buses operate frequently from major cities and towns.

By Rail

Hassan Junction (HAS) has trains from Bengaluru, Mysuru, Mangaluru, and other Karnataka hubs. Autos and taxis are available outside the station.

Nearest Airport

Kannur International Airport (~170–190 km depending on route) and Mangaluru International Airport (~180 km) are common choices. Kempegowda International Airport, Bengaluru is ~240–260 km.

Best Time to Visit

October to February offers pleasant weather for temple exploration and hill views. Monsoon months bring lush greens and higher reservoir levels—great for photography, with rain gear.

Festivals

Watch for temple fairs and Jain festivals at Shravanabelagola; the Mahamastakabhisheka occurs every 12 years.

Local Food

Enjoy Karnataka staples like benne dosa, idli-vada, puliyogare, akki rotti, and meals served on plantain leaves. Try local filter coffee and regional sweets.

Etiquette

Temples require modest dress; leave footwear outside and maintain silence in sanctums.

Stay Options

Hassan town, Belur, Halebidu, and Sakleshpur have hotels, homestays, and resorts across budgets. Book in advance during peak season and festivals.

Getting Around

Local buses, autos, and cabs connect major attractions. For flexibility, consider hiring a cab for a full-day circuit (Belur–Halebidu or Shravanabelagola routes).
